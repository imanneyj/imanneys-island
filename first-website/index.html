The "Meep" Phenomenon

The word "meep" originated as an onomatopoeia for a high-pitched Its popular usage stems primarily from two sources:
Beaker from The Muppets*: Beaker, the nervous lab assistant, communicates almost entirely through high-pitched "Meep" sounds. His "meeps" are used to express surprise, fear, or sadness.
The Road Runner*: The cartoon character's iconic sound is often written as "meep meep," although it is sometimes rendered as "beep beep".
In modern slang, "meep" has become a versatile interjection used to express a wide range of emotions, including awkwardness, excitement, or a general lack of words.
